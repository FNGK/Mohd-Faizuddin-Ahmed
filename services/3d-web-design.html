3D & Immersive Web Design

3D and immersive web that still loads fast.

Most 3D websites make one of two mistakes: they are gorgeous and unusably slow, or they are fast and forgettable. The craft is getting both, a first impression people remember that still respects a mobile connection. The interactive globe on my homepage is a live example of exactly that balance.

In short: I design and build 3D and immersive web experiences with WebGL and Three.js — interactive heroes, product showcases, and launches — engineered with performance budgets and graceful fallbacks so they stay fast and accessible.

Is this the right call for you?

I would rather point you at the right tool than sell you the wrong one. Here is when this fits, and when it does not.

Great fit when…

  • You want a standout first impression competitors cannot copy cheaply

Questions

Common questions

Will 3D make my site slow?

It will if it is done carelessly, which is why I treat performance as part of the design. The 3D loads only on capable devices, lazily, with a strict budget, and everyone else gets a fast, elegant fallback. My own homepage globe never even downloads its 3D library on mobile.

Does 3D work on mobile?

Yes, when it is built responsibly. I gate heavy WebGL to capable devices and serve a lightweight fallback elsewhere, so mobile users get a fast, polished experience rather than a stutter.

Is 3D bad for SEO?

Not if the real content lives in HTML, not inside the canvas. I keep text, headings, and structure fully crawlable and use the 3D as an enhancement, so you get the wow factor without hiding anything from search engines.
